Life Tips

Small habits that make your busy life significantly easier.

🌙
The Night Before Rule
Your mornings are rushed. Do everything at night. This removes 90% of bad food decisions.
  • Pack your lunchbox (10 mins)
  • Prepare your snack zip-lock bag
  • Fill water bottle and leave it at the door
  • Put your workout clothes out (no morning excuse)
  • Set alarm 5 mins earlier than you think you need
💧
The Water Trick
Dehydration feels exactly like hunger. You're probably dehydrated most of the day. Drink 3–3.5 litres daily.
  • 2 glasses immediately after Fajr — before anything
  • Keep 1.5L bottle with you always on commute
  • Drink 1 glass before every meal — reduces how much you eat
  • When you want a snack, drink water first. Wait 10 mins.
📱
Apps That Help
  • Apple Health — already on iPhone, counts steps automatically
  • MyFitnessPal — scan food barcodes to count calories effortlessly
  • Reminders app — set 9:00 PM reminder "Kitchen Closed 🔒"
  • Alarm — label it "Fajr + Water + Workout"
  • Weigh every Monday morning, same time. Log in Notes app.
🚇
Use Your Commute Smartly
3 hours commuting daily is not wasted — it's your best quiet time.
  • Metro: health/fitness podcasts — transforms your mindset over weeks
  • Walk fast everywhere — every step counts toward 10K goal
  • Never stop at petrol station snacks or vending machines
  • Nut pack + dates always in bag — emergency food sorted
  • Use commute to mentally plan next day's meals
🧠
Mindset — Most Important
You're 20, studying, working, commuting 3 hrs daily. You're doing more than most your age. Be proud.
  • You don't need to be perfect. Just 80% consistent.
  • One bad meal doesn't ruin your week — next meal is what counts
  • Weigh weekly not daily — daily fluctuations are just water weight
  • Take measurements every 3 weeks — inches drop even when scale is slow
  • Tell your uncle and aunt what you're doing — their support helps massively
  • Start today. Not Monday. Today.
🍽️
Eating Out in Dubai
Sometimes you'll eat outside. Order smart without guilt.
  • Subway: Wheat bread, chicken/tuna, all veggies, mustard only — no mayo
  • Shawarma: Chicken only, no garlic sauce, no fries, add salad
  • Any restaurant: Grilled chicken, salad, water not juice, no fries
  • Supermarket grab: Greek yogurt + fruit + your boiled egg from bag
  • Always ask: "Can I get the sauce on the side?" — then use very little

🏷️
How to Read Food Labels
  • Always check per 100g calories — not "per serving" (serving sizes are tricks)
  • For yogurt: pick one with less than 5g sugar per 100g
  • For bread: first ingredient must be "whole wheat flour" not just "wheat flour"
  • "Low fat" often means high sugar — always check both numbers
  • Ingredients listed by weight — if sugar is in top 3, put it back
  • Avoid anything with "partially hydrogenated" in ingredients — that's trans fat
  • For peanut butter: ingredients list should say peanuts only — nothing else
